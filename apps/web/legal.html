The Fine Print

Tariffs, terms & disclosures · first edition · subject to revision

The Management records all calls. It always has. The word we are obliged to use is quality assurance.

Plainly, out of character

The rest of this company speaks in character. This page does not. Here is exactly what happens when you call, in plain words.

Who runs this

Elsewhere Telephone Co. is a small interactive-fiction project. The voices you reach are AI personas — not real people, not a real afterlife, and not a helpline. It is entertainment.

What we collect when you call

  • Your phone number (from caller ID), which is how a returning caller is recognized.
  • A recording of the call, both sides.
  • A text transcript of what was said.

What we do with it

Right now, one thing only: we listen back ourselves to make the personas better and check the system works. We do not sell it, share it, train outside models on it, or publish any recording. We will never make a call public without asking you first, on that specific call.

How long we keep it, and getting it deleted

We keep recordings and transcripts while we are building. If you want yours deleted, email the address below from (or naming) the number you called from, and we will remove it. No account, no forms.

The rules of the line

  • You must be 18 or older to call.
  • Don't share real personal data over the line — no SSNs, no card numbers, no addresses. The personas are told to refuse it.
  • Calls are capped at five minutes. Rates from your own carrier may apply; we don't charge you.
  • It's fiction and comedy. Nothing said on the line is medical, legal, financial, or safety advice. If you're in real distress, please contact a real service in your area.
  • We may change, pause, or withdraw any line at any time, and update this page as the project grows.

Reaching a human

Questions, deletions, or anything else: hello@elsewheretel.co.
