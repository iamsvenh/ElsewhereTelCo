A note, out of character

Hi. If you're reading this, you probably called a strange number and want to know what you stumbled into. So, plainly, no fiction:

Elsewhere Telephone Co. is an interactive-fiction company whose medium is the telephone.

You call a number. It connects you to a place — and to whoever lives there. A whole world, explored entirely by voice, through old phones. The personas aren't the product; they're doorways into it.

I build it at night. It's the opposite of my day job and I'm a little obsessed.

— Sven

Why build this
Why only voice?

A world you can only hear — through a handset, eyes free — is more intimate than any screen. And nobody is building it.

Why phones?

A vintage phone is the perfect door: obsolete as technology, perfect as a threshold. You already know exactly how to use it.

Why worlds, not characters?

Novelty wears off. People don't revisit a punchline — they revisit a place. A gag persona decays; a world you can get lost in keeps its pull.
