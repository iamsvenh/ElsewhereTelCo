# Go-to-market & pre-launch traction

**Status: v1 capture for Sven's redline, 2026-07-23.** How we drive traction as early as possible, how we use the (806) signup line without giving the game away, and the pitch deck(s) we turn "what this is" into. Marketing *inside* the fiction is specced in `vision-world-company.md` §11; this is the outside-the-fiction go-to-market layer.

## 1. The tension

Two goals that pull against each other:

- **Stay mysterious.** The landing page and teaser are deliberately vague — a phone number, a wink, an ARG-shaped invitation. That mystery is a feature; it self-selects the diggers and sharers who become the core.
- **Make a splash.** To announce "this is a thing that's coming," we need reach beyond organic word-of-mouth, and we need materials that explain enough to excite without collapsing the mystery.

The resolution is not to pick one. It's to run **mystery as the marketing** and let *explanation* live only in the rooms where it's needed (press, partners, investors) — never on the public surface.

## 2. Mystery-as-marketing (the ARG playbook)

The lineage is ilovebees / Cicada 3301 (vision §12): the fiction leaks into reality and the audience does the spreading. Elsewhere is ARG-native because **phone numbers are reality-native** — a number is inherently mysterious and inherently shareable ("call this, tell me what happens"). Levers:

- **The number is the ad.** A bare number on a sticker, a poster, a classified ad, the desert phone — no explanation. Curiosity does the work.
- **The (806) 666-1212 signup line is the conversion mechanism, and we already built it.** It captures interested callers in-fiction (press 1 to be notified) and becomes the broadcast channel when World One opens ("your line to the Underworld is connected. Dial ___" — vision §11). The pipe exists; we just feed it.
- **Loud-vague / silent-specific split** (vision §11): tell the list *that* there's more (in-fiction service bulletins); make them *find* what (buried extensions, new numbers). You're told there's more; you have to dig for the specifics.

## 3. Channels (early, cheap, on-thesis)

- **Micro-communities that already have the taste:** audio-drama fans (Night Vale, Magnus Archives), ARG communities, immersive-theater / Meow Wolf audiences, weird-fiction readers. Small, high-fit, high-share. Seed here, not broadly.
- **Press hook:** the collision "AI + a real vintage phone + Night Vale-shaped weird fiction, and the Devil actually answers." The physical artifact makes it photographable, which voice-only otherwise isn't.
- **Physical / stunt:** stickers and posters bearing only a number; the desert phone as a PR object (an ARG node by birth); venue placards. The IRL axis (vision §6) is a marketing surface, not just a product surface.
- **The seed list → sharing loop:** Stage 0's whole metric is *unprompted sharing*. The earliest "channel" is the seed testers and whether they spread it unprompted. That signal, not spend, is the green light for everything else.

## 4. The pitch deck(s)

"Everything we're working on right now is defining what this is" — that definition becomes the deck. It is **not one deck**; different rooms need different truths, and the public one must protect the mystery.

| Deck | Audience | Job | Tone | Reveals |
| ---- | -------- | --- | ---- | ------- |
| **Public / press teaser** | the world, journalists, the curious | create anticipation and signups | mysterious, evocative, in-fiction | almost nothing — a number, a feeling, "coming" |
| **Investor deck** | angels / VCs / strategic partners | fund it | analytical + one uncanny video | the full thesis: media-company framing, retention data, economics, moat, roadmap, the ask ([fundraising.md](fundraising.md)) |
| **Partner / strategic deck** | Gimlet-lineage co's, venues, Meow Wolf-shaped, Artizen | collaborate / place / license | collaborative, IP-forward | the world's depth and the collaboration surface; less on unit economics |

The uncanny 60-second **video** (fundraising §3) is the shared asset across the investor and partner decks — voice-only doesn't pitch on a static slide.

**The public/press teaser deck is realized as a web page, not slides:** `elsewheretel.co/directory` (hidden — not linked from the landing nav, `noindex`, shareable by URL for 1:1 follow-ups). Framed in-world as the opening pages of the Directory of Elsewhere, with a deliberate two-voice split — elegant in-fiction beats vs. a typed-memo honest section ("what this really is / where we are / why"). It's the web version of the friend reveal-reply: the thing you send after someone's intrigued but before they'd read the docs.

Every deliberate push into one of these channels gets logged in **[outreach-log.md](outreach-log.md)** — exact copy, intent, and the `/teaser-stats` window after — so each send teaches us something instead of vanishing.

## 5. Discipline gate

From the vision: **build the announcement pipe (cheap, we have it); don't build a marketing machine before the sharing signal exists. Leak, don't spray.** The decks and the traction plan are cheap to *prepare* now and worth having ready — but the *splash* fires after the call is proven fun and shared, not before. Preparing ≠ launching.
