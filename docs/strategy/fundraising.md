## 1. Thesis — why this is fundable

Elsewhere is not "an AI app." It is a **media / world company** whose medium happens to be the telephone and whose production cost happens to be bent low by a diegetic cost architecture. The fundable story:

- **A new-form media company** in a lineage investors already understand and have paid for: narrative audio (podcasts), immersive experience (Meow Wolf), interactive fiction, ARG.
- **A compounding moat that isn't the model.** Canon accreted from thousands of real calls is IP no competitor reproduces with the same API (vision §1, §10). The moat is the world, not the weights.
- **Retention over novelty.** The whole reframe attacks the documented #1 risk (novelty decay) by selling *places people revisit*, not punchlines.
- **A believable margin path.** The live-AI ratio + self-hosting seam is a COGS story with a roadmap, not a hand-wave (business-model §4).

## 2. Comps and the strategic-partner angle (Sven, 2026-07-23)

The parallel Sven draws: **Gimlet Media**. A media company that built narrative audio into a fundable, ultimately acquirable business — and famously documented *its own founding and fundraising* in the *StartUp* podcast (season 1 walked through pitching investors, including brutal on-mic feedback from Chris Sacca; Gimlet was later acquired by Spotify, reportedly ~\$230M, in 2019). *(Verify all figures/names before external use.)*

The insight: companies like this are **always looking for the next form** of storytelling. We draw the Night Vale / immersive-theater parallels already; Elsewhere is those, but *interactive* and *two-way*, which one-directional audio is not. That makes the Gimlet-lineage companies (and their acquirers) not just comps but **potential strategic partners or acquirers** — and their backers **potential investors**.

Two ways to approach the money:

1. **Strategic media companies** themselves — narrative-audio / immersive-experience studios hunting for the next interactive form. Partnership, co-production, or acquisition framing.
2. **The investors who funded them** — media / entertainment-tech / creator-economy VCs and the angels active in narrative audio and immersive experience. They already have the thesis; we fit a pattern they've paid for before.

Comp set to research and name properly for a deck:
- **Narrative audio:** the Gimlet lineage and its peers/successors; the funds and angels behind them.
- **Immersive / experiential:** Meow Wolf (also a potential *customer* — physical portals into our universe) and its investor base. **Ministry of Awe** (Philadelphia — per a tester, a Meow Wolf-like immersive venue already doing phone-based experiences): research as a closer-scale comp/inspiration and possible precedent for the medium.
- **Interactive fiction / AI-native storytelling:** the current wave; who's funding it and on what thesis.
- **Artizen:** testing pool AND funding fit (match rounds, art-drop launch framing) AND future collaborators for the eventual visual layer (vision §8).

## 3. The artifact that raises

Per the vision: **the fundraising artifact is retention evidence + the video, not lore volume.** What we bring to a room:

- Hard retention signal: repeat-call rate, call length, the lore-probe rate (callers asking "what's the Management?" = they understood the product), and unprompted sharing.
- A short, uncanny **video** that makes someone *feel* the thing in 60 seconds (a real person, a vintage handset, the Devil answering). Voice-only is hard to pitch on a slide; the video does the work a screenshot can't.
- The world itself as evidence of the moat (canon depth, the frame's coherence).

Not: a pile of unread lore. Depth matters only insofar as it drives the retention numbers.

## 4. When to raise

Gated by the same signal that gates everything: **the call has to be provably fun and shared first.** Sequence:

- **Now → Stage 0:** no raise. Prove the call is fun and gets shared (the kill criterion still stands). Prep the *materials* (this doc, the deck) cheaply in parallel.
- **Post-fun signal (retention exists):** the story becomes tellable. This is when the strategic/partner conversations and any angel/pre-seed conversation can start — with numbers, not vibes.
- **Scale:** a larger raise is the story of "media company at scale" — more districts, the lore engine, self-hosted inference as a margin lever, the ARG/licensing surface (vision §13, stage 3-4).

Discipline: don't raise on the vision alone. The vision is the shape; the retention data is the proof; raise on the proof.
