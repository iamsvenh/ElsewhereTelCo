# The Devil — visual aesthetic (canonical)

**Status: LOCKED 2026-07-24.** The Devil's canonical *look*, derived from the "Call Me Maybe" poster series (v1–v3, in this folder). Voice-only product, so this governs the *visual* side — posters, merch, classified ads, the eventual visual atlas — not the calls. Every future depiction draws this same face. Hero reference: **v3** (`devil-call-me-maybe-v3.png`).

## The look

Not a cartoon devil — **a weary mid-century office clerk who happens to be the Devil.** Middle-management-of-Hell energy (canon: a bored functionary with cosmic authority, DMV-clerk amusement). Human enough to almost trust; the wrongness is in the details.

- **Who:** a handsome-ish, tired man in his 40s. Slicked-back dark hair, sharp **widow's peak**, clean-shaven.
- **Face / expression:** half-lidded eyes with a **faint amber-red inner glow**; a **cold, sly, one-sided smirk** — sinister-charming, amused at your expense. Deadpan menace. *Never* a warm/friendly grin.
- **Wardrobe:** 1950s office — white shirt, **sleeves rolled up**, **necktie loosened**, no jacket. Working late, slightly rumpled.
- **The one cigarette:** a **single** lit cigarette **held in his hand**, raised near the face, a thin curl of smoke. (Never two; never one in the mouth *and* one in an ashtray — that ambiguity was the main v1/v2 miss.)
- **Setting:** a dim, cluttered office — a desk of intake paperwork, a **black rotary telephone** at his elbow, a **banker's desk lamp** glowing amber. Film-noir mood.
- **The signature device (the whole gag):** his cast **SHADOW on the wall has curved horns and a pointed tail** his human body doesn't. This is the *only* overt sign of what he is. It recurs on every piece. No costume horns, no red skin, no pitchfork.
- **Palette:** warm dark + saturated glowing **amber-orange** (our neon amber) + **cream** + deep **black** + a bruise of **maroon-red**. Bathed in amber lamp glow.
- **Medium:** vintage mid-century **advertising-illustration** style — inked comic/poster linework, halftone print texture, aged/worn cream paper, subtle ink misregistration. Classy and deadpan, never kitschy.
- **Typography (on posters):** Playfair-style serif display headline with a **maroon-red accent word**; Special-Elite-style **typewriter** face for officialese small print; the phone number spaced **(806) 666 1212**; the "A SERVICE OF THE ELSEWHERE TELEPHONE CO." tag line.

## Do / Don't

- **Do:** human, weary, sinister-charming, amber-lit; the horned shadow; one cigarette in hand; deadpan.
- **Don't:** red skin, costume horns, pitchfork, goatee/Van Dyke cliché, cartoon, a warm/friendly grin, garish or kitschy.
